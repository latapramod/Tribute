Mother Teresa

"..... Do things for people not because of who they are or what they do in return, but because of who you are."

Biography

  • Born : 26 August 1910 in Skopje.
  • Lived for most of her life in India
    .Other names:Saint Teresa of Calcutta.
  • Teresa left home in 1928 at age 18 to join the Sisters of Loreto.
  • She was a teacher at the Loreto convent school in Entally, eastern Calcutta.
  • Died on 5 September 1997 (aged 87) Calcutta, West Bengal, India.

Achievements

  • Teresa was first recognised by the Indian government receiving the Padma Shri in 1962.
  • She recieved Bharat Ratna
    (India's highest civilian award) in 1980.
  • Teresa received the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Peace and International Understanding.
  • In 1979, Teresa received the
    Nobel Peace Prize.

Charities

  • In 1950 Teresa founded the Missionaries of Charity.
  • By 1996, Teresa operated 517 missions in over 100 countries.
  • Teresa travelled to assist the hungry in Ethiopia.
  • The first Missionaries of Charity home in the United States was established in the South Bronx area of New York City.
  • 1957 they began to work with lepers.
